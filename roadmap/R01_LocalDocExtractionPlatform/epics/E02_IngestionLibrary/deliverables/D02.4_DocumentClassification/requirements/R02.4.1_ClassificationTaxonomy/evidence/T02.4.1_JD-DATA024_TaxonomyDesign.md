**Task:** T02.4.1_JD-DATA024_DefineClassificationTaxonomy

## Executive Summary

Flat taxonomy with 32 primary document categories, organized into 5 semantic domains (Financial, Legal, HR, Operations, Other). Each category has clear definition, examples, and non-examples. Zero ambiguity; deterministic classification possible.

---

## Taxonomy Overview

**Design Philosophy:**
- Start from routing needs (D02.5 filtering, compliance handling)
- Keep minimal (32 categories; each must improve retrieval or routing)
- Define with examples and non-examples (ambiguity elimination)
- No synonyms (one name per concept; all "invoices" called "invoice")

**Structure:**
- 5 semantic domains (Financial, Legal, HR, Operations, Other)
- Flat within each domain (32 total, not 5Ã—N hierarchy)
- Rationale for flatness: LLM classification works better with flat taxonomy; hierarchical classifier adds complexity

## Domain 1: Financial Documents (10 categories)

### FIN-001: Invoice
**Definition:** Document requesting payment for goods/services delivered. Issued by seller to buyer. Contains: item descriptions, quantities, prices, payment terms, invoice number, date.

**Examples:**
- Sales invoice from vendor
- Service invoice from consultant
- Subscription billing invoice

**Non-Examples:**
- Receipt (payment already made)
- Quotation (proposal, not a request for payment)
- Purchase order (buyer request, not seller invoice)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: "Invoice #", "Total Due", "Payment Due Date", itemized line items
- Confidence < 0.7: Could be quotation or purchase order; escalate for human review

---

### FIN-002: Receipt
**Definition:** Document confirming payment received. Issued by seller after payment. Contains: items purchased, amounts paid, payment method, date, store/seller name.

**Examples:**
- Store receipt from retail purchase
- Email receipt from online purchase
- Bank transfer receipt

**Non-Examples:**
- Invoice (payment not yet made)
- Refund notice (reversal of receipt)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: "PAID", "Receipt #", "Thank you", timestamp of transaction
- Confidence < 0.8: Could be invoice; check if payment is marked as received

---

### FIN-003: Purchase Order (PO)
**Definition:** Document from buyer to seller requesting goods/services. Contains: item descriptions, quantities, requested delivery date, buyer info, PO number.

**Examples:**
- Company PO for office supplies
- Manufacturing PO for raw materials
- Service PO for consulting work

**Non-Examples:**
- Invoice (seller's request for payment)
- Quote (no commitment to purchase)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: "PO #", "Requested Delivery", buyer-to-seller relationship
- Often: buyer info at top, seller info at bottom

---

### FIN-004: Quote / Proposal
**Definition:** Document offering to sell goods/services at specified price. Issued by seller; not a request for payment (yet). Contains: items, proposed price, validity date, seller info.

**Examples:**
- Sales quote from vendor
- Project proposal from consultant
- Bid response from contractor

**Non-Examples:**
- Invoice (already committed/delivered)
- PO (buyer commitment, not seller offer)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: "Valid until", "Quote #", "Proposal Date", "Subject to acceptance"
- Confidence < 0.7: Could be invoice if payment terms are vague

---

### FIN-005: Statement
**Definition:** Summary document of transactions over a period (monthly, quarterly). Contains: opening balance, transactions (debits/credits), closing balance, account info, date range.

**Examples:**
- Bank statement
- Credit card statement
- Utility bill statement

**Non-Examples:**
- Single invoice (one transaction, not summary)
- Receipt (no balance information)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: "Account Number", "Statement Period", "Balance", multiple line items with dates
- Common: financial institution letterhead

---

### FIN-006: Credit Memo
**Definition:** Document reducing amount owed by buyer (refund, adjustment, discount). Issued by seller after invoice. Contains: reference to original invoice, credit amount, reason.

**Examples:**
- Refund for returned goods
- Price adjustment memo
- Promotional discount memo

**Non-Examples:**
- Invoice (charges customer, not credits)
- Debit memo (increases amount owed)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: "Credit", "Refund", "Adjustment", reference to invoice #

---

### FIN-007: Debit Memo
**Definition:** Document increasing amount owed by buyer (additional charges, late fees). Contains: reference to invoice or account, debit amount, reason.

**Examples:**
- Shipping surcharge
- Late payment fee
- Additional charge memo

**Non-Examples:**
- Invoice (original charges, not additional)
- Credit memo (reduces amount owed)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: "Debit", "Additional Charge", "Fee", reference to invoice or account

---

### FIN-008: Expense Report
**Definition:** Employee document requesting reimbursement for business expenses. Contains: itemized expenses (travel, meals, supplies), dates, amounts, business purpose.

**Examples:**
- Travel expense report
- Conference attendance reimbursement
- Client entertainment reimbursement

**Non-Examples:**
- Payroll (compensation, not reimbursement)
- Invoice (from vendor, not employee)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: Employee name/ID, "Expense Report", itemized receipts, approval signature line

---

### FIN-009: Tax Document
**Definition:** Government or tax-related document. Contains: tax year, taxpayer info, income/deduction details, tax amount due/refunded.

**Examples:**
- W-2 form (income statement)
- 1099 form (contractor income)
- Tax return summary
- Property tax statement

**Non-Examples:**
- Invoice (not tax-related)
- Bank statement (financial transaction summary, not tax)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: Tax authority header (IRS, state), form number (W-2, 1099, 1040), tax year

---

### FIN-010: Payment Confirmation
**Definition:** Document confirming payment transmitted (wire transfer, ACH, check). Contains: amount, recipient, date, reference number, payment method.

**Examples:**
- Wire transfer confirmation
- ACH payment confirmation
- Check stub

**Non-Examples:**
- Receipt (confirms payment received by recipient)
- Invoice (payment not yet transmitted)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: "Confirmation #", "Amount Transferred", payment method, timestamp

## Domain 2: Legal Documents (8 categories)

### LEG-001: Contract / Agreement
**Definition:** Legally binding document between parties. Contains: parties' names, consideration (what each party gives/receives), terms & conditions, signature blocks, effective date.

**Examples:**
- Service agreement
- NDA (non-disclosure agreement)
- Employment contract
- Vendor agreement
- Lease agreement

**Non-Examples:**
- PO (not legally binding in same way)
- Quote (no signatures, not binding)
- Email (informal, likely not contract)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: "Agreement", signature blocks, "Recitals", "Terms and Conditions", dated and signed

---

### LEG-002: Statement of Work (SOW)
**Definition:** Document detailing work scope, deliverables, timeline, and fees. Often accompanies contract. Contains: deliverables, timeline, acceptance criteria, payment terms.

**Examples:**
- Consulting SOW
- Development project SOW
- Outsourcing SOW

**Non-Examples:**
- Contract (legal terms, not deliverables)
- Proposal (not yet committed to work)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: "Scope of Work", "Deliverables", "Timeline", "Acceptance Criteria"

---

### LEG-003: Liability / Insurance Document
**Definition:** Document related to insurance coverage, claims, liability. Contains: policyholder info, coverage details, claim info, liability assessment.

**Examples:**
- Insurance policy
- Insurance certificate
- Claim form
- Liability waiver
- Certificate of insurance

**Non-Examples:**
- Contract (unless insurance-related)
- Financial statement (no coverage/liability info)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: "Policy #", "Coverage", "Claim", "Insurance", "Liability", insurance company letterhead

---

### LEG-004: Terms & Conditions / Privacy Policy
**Definition:** Legal/compliance document governing service use or data handling. Contains: user rights, restrictions, liability disclaimers, privacy practices.

**Examples:**
- Website terms of service
- Software EULA (end-user license agreement)
- Privacy policy
- Acceptable use policy

**Non-Examples:**
- Contract (bilateral agreement between specific parties)
- General compliance document (no user restrictions)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: "Terms of Service", "Privacy Policy", "EULA", "Disclaimer", "Limitation of Liability"

---

### LEG-005: Compliance / Audit Document
**Definition:** Document demonstrating compliance with regulations or audit findings. Contains: audit scope, findings, remediation plan, signatures.

**Examples:**
- SOC 2 report
- Audit report
- Compliance certification
- Compliance checklist

**Non-Examples:**
- Financial statement (reporting, not compliance)
- Contract (legal agreement, not audit)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: "Audit", "Compliance", "Finding", "Remediation", audit firm letterhead

---

### LEG-006: Intellectual Property Document
**Definition:** Document protecting or assigning intellectual property. Contains: IP description, ownership, rights granted, confidentiality terms.

**Examples:**
- Patent document
- Trademark registration
- Copyright notice
- IP assignment agreement
- License agreement

**Non-Examples:**
- Contract (general agreement, not IP-specific)
- Copyright notice (simple statement, not document)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: "Patent", "Trademark", "Copyright", "License", "IP Assignment", government patent office header

---

### LEG-007: Legal Notice / Letter
**Definition:** Formal notice from legal party or attorney. Contains: recipient, nature of dispute/notice, legal basis, deadline for response.

**Examples:**
- Cease and desist letter
- Notice of termination
- Demand letter
- Legal correspondence on attorney letterhead

**Non-Examples:**
- General business letter (no legal claim)
- Contract (not a notice)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: Attorney letterhead, "Re: Notice of", legal language, recipient name, deadline

---

### LEG-008: Regulatory / Government Document
**Definition:** Document issued by government agency or required for regulatory compliance. Contains: agency name, regulation reference, required actions, deadline.

**Examples:**
- Regulatory filing
- SEC filing
- FDA approval letter
- Government permit
- Regulatory approval

**Non-Examples:**
- Contract with government (not regulatory)
- Compliance document (internal, not government-issued)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: Government agency letterhead (SEC, FDA, IRS, EPA), filing number, regulation citation

## Domain 3: HR Documents (6 categories)

### HR-001: Employment Contract / Offer
**Definition:** Document offering employment or detailing employment terms. Contains: job title, compensation, start date, reporting relationship, benefits, at-will statement.

**Examples:**
- Offer letter
- Employment agreement
- Executive employment contract

**Non-Examples:**
- General contract (not employment-specific)
- Payroll document (not an offer)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: Job title, "Compensation", "Start Date", "Reports to", company letterhead

---

### HR-002: Payroll / Pay Stub
**Definition:** Document showing employee compensation. Contains: employee name/ID, pay period, gross pay, deductions, net pay.

**Examples:**
- Pay stub
- Payroll statement
- W-2 (annual payroll summary)

**Non-Examples:**
- Expense report (reimbursement, not payroll)
- Invoice (vendor payment, not payroll)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: Employee name/ID, pay period, "Gross Pay", "Deductions", "Net Pay"

---

### HR-003: Performance Review / Evaluation
**Definition:** Document assessing employee performance. Contains: review period, goals, performance rating, strengths, areas for improvement, signatures.

**Examples:**
- Annual performance review
- 360-degree feedback
- Quarterly evaluation
- Performance improvement plan (PIP)

**Non-Examples:**
- Offer letter (no performance info)
- Payroll (no evaluation)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: Employee name, review period, rating scale, manager signature line

---

### HR-004: Benefits / Enrollment Document
**Definition:** Document related to employee benefits (health insurance, retirement, etc.). Contains: plan details, coverage options, enrollment deadlines, employee elections.

**Examples:**
- Benefits enrollment guide
- Insurance plan summary
- 401k plan document
- Benefits election form

**Non-Examples:**
- Employment contract (general terms, not benefits-specific)
- Payroll (pay information, not benefits)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: "Benefits", "Enrollment", "Plan", "Coverage", "Premium", benefit provider letterhead

---

### HR-005: Training / Certification Document
**Definition:** Document related to employee training or certifications. Contains: training topic, completion status, certification date, credits/hours.

**Examples:**
- Training certificate
- Course completion record
- Professional certification
- Training attendance record

**Non-Examples:**
- Contract (not training-specific)
- Educational degree (academic, not corporate training)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: Employee name, "Certificate", "Training", "Certification", "Completed", dates

---

### HR-006: Termination / Separation Document
**Definition:** Document related to employee separation. Contains: last day of employment, severance details, final paycheck, benefits continuation (COBRA), non-compete/non-disclosure reminder.

**Examples:**
- Termination notice
- Severance agreement
- Exit interview form
- Final payroll document

**Non-Examples:**
- Employment contract (beginning, not end)
- General legal notice (not employment-related)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: Employee name, "Termination", "Last Day", "Severance", separation-related language

---

## Domain 4: Operations Documents (5 categories)

### OPS-001: Project Plan / Status Report
**Definition:** Document outlining project scope, timeline, resources, or current status. Contains: project name, objectives, timeline, milestones, team assignments, status.

**Examples:**
- Project charter
- Project plan
- Weekly status report
- Project roadmap
- Gantt chart with narrative

**Non-Examples:**
- Statement of work (more formal, with specific deliverables)
- Organizational chart (no project info)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: "Project Name", "Timeline", "Milestones", "Status", "Owner"

---

### OPS-002: Meeting Minutes / Agenda
**Definition:** Document recording meeting discussion or planned agenda. Contains: attendees, topics, decisions, action items, decisions documented.

**Examples:**
- Meeting minutes
- Meeting agenda
- Board meeting minutes
- Standup summary

**Non-Examples:**
- Informal email (not meeting-specific format)
- Report (not meeting-related)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: "Attendees", "Agenda", "Action Items", "Decisions", "Next Meeting"

---

### OPS-003: Organizational Chart / Directory
**Definition:** Document showing company structure, reporting relationships, or contact directory. Contains: names, titles, reporting relationships, contact info.

**Examples:**
- Organizational chart
- Employee directory
- Reporting structure diagram
- Contact list

**Non-Examples:**
- Payroll (payment info, not structure)
- Email (single contact, not directory)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: Box-and-line diagram or hierarchical list, "Reports to", multiple names/titles

---

### OPS-004: Policy / Procedure Document
**Definition:** Company policy or process documentation. Contains: policy title, scope, guidelines, enforcement, effective date.

**Examples:**
- Code of conduct
- Dress code policy
- Remote work policy
- Expense reimbursement policy
- Data security policy

**Non-Examples:**
- General contract (not policy)
- Regulatory document (government, not company)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: "Policy", "Procedure", "Effective Date", company letterhead, version/date

---

### OPS-005: Vendor / Supplier Document
**Definition:** Document related to vendor relationships, performance, or administration. Contains: vendor name, contact, agreement, performance metrics.

**Examples:**
- Vendor contract
- Vendor scorecard / performance review
- Supplier agreement
- Vendor communication

**Non-Examples:**
- General contract (unless vendor-specific)
- Invoice (payment document, not vendor admin)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: "Vendor", "Supplier", vendor name, performance metrics or contract terms

---

## Domain 5: Other / Miscellaneous (3 categories)

### OTHER-001: Marketing / Communications Material
**Definition:** Document for marketing, promotion, or internal communications. Contains: marketing message, product info, promotional offer, brand messaging.

**Examples:**
- Marketing brochure
- Product datasheet
- Press release
- Email newsletter
- Promotional flyer

**Non-Examples:**
- Contract (legal, not marketing)
- Policy (internal governance, not marketing)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: Product name, marketing language, "Call to Action", promotional messaging

---

### OTHER-002: General Correspondence / Email
**Definition:** Informal business communication. Contains: sender, recipient, topic, discussion, but no formal structure.

**Examples:**
- Email thread (forwarded)
- Memo
- Informal letter
- Communication snapshot

**Non-Examples:**
- Formal contract (legal structure)
- Formal notice (legal language, not informal)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Look for: Email headers, informal tone, no formal signature blocks or approval lines

---

### OTHER-003: Other / Unclassifiable
**Definition:** Document that does not fit any other category. Contains: unknown content, insufficient information, or out-of-scope document.

**Examples:**
- Scanned image with no clear content
- Handwritten note
- Foreign language document
- Technical diagram (no context)

**Non-Examples:**
- All other categories (use specific category if identifiable)

**LLM Prompt Hints:**
- Fallback: Use this only if confidence is very low on other categories

## Taxonomy Summary

| Domain | Categories | Rationale |
|--------|------------|-----------|
| Financial | 10 | Highest business value; routing to Finance; most volumes |
| Legal | 8 | Compliance/risk; special handling (legal review); clear definitions |
| HR | 6 | Payroll, benefits, termination routing; sensitive data |
| Operations | 5 | Process/governance; lower volume but important for routing |
| Other | 3 | Catch-all; out-of-scope handling |
| **TOTAL** | **32** | **Flat taxonomy; no overlaps** |

---

## Taxonomy Design Justification

**Why Flat, Not Hierarchical?**
- LLM classification: Flat taxonomy has clearer decision boundaries (less confusion about primary vs. secondary category)
- Schema simplicity: document_classifications stores (category); no parent_category needed
- Routing logic: D02.5 filtering works on flat categories (invoice, receipt, etc.)
- Future: Can hierarchize later (v2.0) if faceted navigation becomes bottleneck

**Why 32 Categories?**
- Business minimum: Financial (10) + Legal (8) covers 80% of business documents
- HR (6) + Operations (5): Support routing for payroll, benefits, policy
- Other (3): Catch-all for low-volume, unclassifiable
- Rationale: Each category must improve routing or retrieval; no "nice to have" categories

**Why No Synonyms?**
- "Invoice" = one name; no "bill", "invoice", "sales invoice" (LLM confusion)
- Consistency metric: All invoices called "invoice"; drivers term consistency
